OEM Advanced Line

OE FIX & RESTORATION

The OEM Advanced Line represents an OE FIX Evolution. Factory components are fundamentally engineered around strict volume cost-saving constraints, resulting in compromised materials, simplified internal geometries, and reduced service life.

Our Tier-1 engineering department systematically analyzes specific original equipment failure modes using destructive testing and field return data. We completely reverse-engineer every physical vulnerability and deploy superior, direct-fit solutions designed to outlast and outperform the original chassis components — while maintaining 100% dimensional compatibility for zero-modification installation.

Failure Mode Analysis

Boot Degradation → CR Multi-Lip

OE natural rubber tears under thermal cycling. Grit forms destructive grinding paste inside. Our Chloroprene (CR) resists ozone. Multi-lip profile dynamically locks out water and debris.

Alloy Shearing → 42CrMo4 Metallurgy

OE carbon steels experience rapid metallurgical fatigue under kinetic stress. Induction-hardened 42CrMo4 increases tensile strength to 1000 MPa.

Bearing Play → POM Polymer Matrix

OE soft plastic bearings rapidly deform under load, creating loose compliance and wandering steering feedback. Low-friction Polyoxymethylene (POM) shells maintain zero play for the entire service life.

The Commercial Advantage

Solid Forged Body Conversion

Weak stamped OE arms upgraded to solid forged bodies with vastly thicker housing flanges. This massive structural rigidity upgrade completely prevents control arms from twisting, bending, or catastrophically folding under heavy payload carry, extreme off-road suspension articulation, or repeated impact from municipal fleet service on damaged road surfaces.

Memory Steer Correction

Solid front axles on heavy-duty trucks develop micro-imperfections over time, creating misaligned mounting surfaces. Standard OE ball joints bind against these imperfections, causing dangerous "memory steer" — where the vehicle pulls persistently after every turn. Our specialized directional/snap-in sintered bearings safely accommodate off-center loads, permanently curing this condition and reducing fleet liability.

Greaseable Maintenance Intervals

Unlike sealed "lifetime" OE joints that trap contaminated grease and accelerate wear, the BULL Series SAE Zerk fittings allow scheduled re-greasing during routine fleet maintenance. This enables predictable maintenance planning, extends service intervals by up to 3× the OE lifecycle, and provides a clear physical indicator of joint condition at each service.

Adjustable Series

LIFT / DROP CORRECTION

When modifying ride height — whether a 3-inch lift for off-road clearance or aggressive lowering for track aero — factory suspension geometry is severely disrupted. Factory arms are cast to strict, immutable lengths that cannot accommodate any deviation from stock ride height without dangerous consequences for drivability, tire wear, and steering safety.

Replacing rigid OE arms with EXEDY FRAP Adjustable Series restores alignment range. Essential for 4×4 Lift Kits and Lowered Track Cars. Our Adjustable line offers complete freedom to dial in precise Camber and Caster settings, restoring drivability, steering feel, and vehicle safety.

On-Vehicle Tuning

External Hex Adjustment (±3°)

Our External Hex Adjustment Sleeves replace slippery round OE profiles with machined hexagonal surfaces. The technician attaches a standard wrench and makes infinitely precise micro-adjustments directly on the vehicle while loaded on the alignment rack. Saves hours of labor.

The Lift Kit Dilemma & Suspension Binding

Raising a vehicle intrinsically alters the operating angle of the factory control arms, pushing both Camber and Caster specifications far beyond OEM acceptable limits. At full suspension droop on a lifted 4×4, the factory upper ball joint can physically bind and shear the ball stud. Our specialized high-angle ball joints safely accommodate extended suspension travel.

Billet T6 Aluminum Construction

CNC-machined from aerospace-grade T6 forged aluminum block. Incredibly strong, exceptionally lightweight (up to 40% lighter than OE stamped steel equivalents) to reduce unsprung mass. Protected by Hard Anodized Type III (Mil-Spec corrosion protection) surface treatment.

Education & Science

Chassis Kinematics Glossary

Essential terminology for advanced chassis dynamics, material science, and steering geometry correction.

Camber Dynamics

Inward/outward vertical tilt of front tires. Incorrect camber forces vehicle weight onto extreme tire edges, causing rapid and uneven inner/outer edge wear. Measured in degrees from vertical. Corrected via Adjustable Series turnbuckles.

Caster Kinematics

Forward/rearward angular tilt of the steering axis pivot line. Positive caster dramatically improves straight-line highway stability and natural steering wheel returnability after cornering. Lift kits destroy positive caster; the Adjustable Series restores it.

Memory Steer Condition

A dangerous condition where the vehicle persistently pulls left or right after completing a turn. Caused by binding joints in misaligned solid truck axles forcing ball joints to operate off-center. Fixed permanently via BULL Series directional sintered bearings.

Kinematic Compliance

Unwanted flexing and compression of suspension components under cornering load, dynamically altering Toe, Camber, and Caster geometry mid-turn. Eliminated by FRAP Rosso PTFE-lined zero-compliance Uniballs.

Sintered Matrix Metallurgy

Advanced powdered metal forming creating a microscopic porous bearing structure. This matrix holds liquid MoS₂ extreme-pressure lubrication inside its structure for continuous self-feeding flow, vastly outlasting solid plastics in heavy-duty towing applications.

Unsprung Mass Reduction

Total weight of all suspension components not supported by the vehicle's springs. Reducing unsprung mass via T6 Billet Aluminum vastly improves damper reaction speed, keeping tires pressed to tarmac over bumps and surface changes.

Breakaway Torque

The precise rotational resistance a ball joint stud exerts before initiating movement. Calibrated in Nm, this value must match OEM specifications exactly — too loose causes steering wander, too tight creates heavy steering feel. Critical for Steer-by-Wire xEV compatibility.

Eddy Current Inspection

Non-destructive electromagnetic testing method that detects sub-surface micro-cracks, voids, and metallurgical discontinuities in forged components invisible to visual or dimensional inspection. Applied at 100% rate on all FRAP safety-critical forged parts directly on the production line.
